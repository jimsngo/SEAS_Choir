=== Entrance Antiphon ===

I Know That My Redeemer Lives
Scott Soper
[Verse 1]
I know that my Redeemer lives, the One who calls me home. I long to see God face to face, to see with my own eyes.
[Refrain]
I know that my Redeemer lives, that I shall rise again.
[Verse 2]
I know that I shall one day see the goodness of the Lord, when God will wipe away our tears, and death will be no more.
[Verse 3]
The last day I shall rise again, shall be remade like God. My home shall be by God's own side, the dying, rising Lord.

=== Responsorial Psalm ===

Psalm 23: 1-3, 3-4, 5, 6
Sarah Hart
[Response]
The Lord is my shepherd; there is nothing I shall want. The Lord is my shepherd; there is nothing I shall want.
[Verse 1]
The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want. In verdant pastures he gives me repose; beside restful waters he leads me; he refreshes my soul.
[Verse 2]
He guides me in right paths for his name’s sake. Even though I walk in the dark valley I fear no evil; for you are at my side with your rod and your staff that give me courage.
[Verse 3]
You spread the table before me in the sight of my foes; you anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows.
[Verse 4]
Only goodness and kindness follow me all the days of my life; and I shall dwell in the house of the LORD for years to come.

=== Offertory ===

Keep Me in Your Heart
Janèt Sullivan Whitaker
[Verse 1]
Jesus, hold me in peace; keep me in your heart.  Let me know your mercy; keep me in your heart.  Jesus, you are my hope. Help me when the darkness comes. In your kindness, keep me in your heart.
[Verse 2]
Jesus, you are my life; keep me in your heart. Fill me with your courage; keep me in your heart.  Sometimes sorrows will come. Jesus, you are always near. In my sadness, keep me in your heart.
[Verse 3]
Jesus, morning will come; keep me in your heart. Raise me with each new day; keep me in your heart. Jesus, teach me to love. Faithful let me always be, ’cause you love me. Keep me in your heart.

Steve Angrisano/Tom Booth
