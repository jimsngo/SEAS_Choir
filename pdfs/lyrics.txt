=== Entrance Antiphon ===

Antiphon # 85
Let Us All Rejoice

We should glory in the Cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, in whom is our salvation, life and resurrection, through whom we are saved.

=== Responsorial Psalm ===

Psalm 78 - Do Not Forget the Works of the Lord
Francesca LaRosa
[Response]
Do not forget the works of the Lord!
[Verse 1]
Hearken, my people, to my teaching; incline your ears to the words of my mouth. I will open my mouth in a parable, I will utter mysteries from of old.
[Verse 2]
While he slew them they sought him and inquired after God again, Remembering that God was their rock and the Most High God, their redeemer.
[Verse 3]
But they flattered him with their mouths and lied to him with their tongues; Though their hearts were not steadfast toward him, nor were they faithful to his covenant.
[Verse 4]
But he, being merciful, forgave their sin and destroyed them not; Often he turned back his anger and let none of his wrath be roused.

=== Offertory ===

With Our Eyes on the Cross
Sarah Hart and Greg Walton/Vocal arr. by Scott Soper
[Verse 1]
Oh, the riches of this life. Oh, the way we have been blessed. Oh, the poverty we own in the things that we posses. You ask us to lay down and the treasure we have stored for the treasure of your kingdom and a narrow road.
[Refrain]
With our eyes on the cross, eyes on the cross, with our eyes on the cross we will see. With our eyes on the cross, eyes on the cross, with our eyes on the cross we will see.
[Verse 2]
We have more than we could need, all our fill of daily bread, but the son of righteousness has no place to lay his he'd. And all this world's a feast, but he draws us to a table where the greatest and the least come as equals.
[Refrain]
[Bridge]
We will see no us or them, no slave or free. We will see all of our sad divisions cease. In his suffering the walls come tumbling down and we become we, with our eyes on the cross.
[Refrain]

=== Communion ===

Freedom In Your Cross
Cooper Ray, Laura Ray, Tom Booth, Curtis Stephan, and Steve Angrisano; Vocal arr. by Scott Soper
[Verse 1]
Deep inside this hardened heart, amid a stormy sea, lies a love that aches for you and longs to be set free, to be set free.
[Verse 2]
You have come to ransom me, release me from these chains. To follow you, Lord, is my gain; I die to rise again.
[Refrain]
There is freedom in your cross, hope for all the lost. The bonds of our shame have fallen away. There is freedom, freedom in your cross.
[Verse 3]
Out of darkness, into light, my eyes now see your truth. I embrace this life you offer me and surrender all to you.
[Refrain]
There is freedom in your cross, hope for all the lost. The bonds of our shame have fallen away. There is freedom, freedom in your cross.
[Bridge]
Your love paid the price; what a great sacrifice. You freed me, you freed me. (Repeat)
You freed me Lord. There is freedom in your cross.

=== Recessional ===

Sweet Redeemer
Steve Angrisano and Sarah Hart
[Verse 1]
King of Kings, blessed Jesus, hand of mercy, giver of grace; I have come to sing glory, to lay my heart before you in this place.
[Chorus]
Sweet redeemer, I surrender all I am to you. Sweet redeemer, I surrender all I am to you.
[Verse 2]
I was lost and you found me; In the darkness, you were my light. When I fell down, you forgave me; Lifted me up and held me to your side. 
[Chorus]
